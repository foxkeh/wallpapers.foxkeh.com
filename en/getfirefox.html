Creating a wallpaper for the browser

Foxkeh's Wallpaper Creator is a tool that helps to create Foxkeh wallpapers easily on modern browser utilizing a web-based technology called SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics). You can select images and background to customize the size and the layout to your liking.

There are three types of wallpapers that can be created using this tool: desktop wallpapers for PCs, standby wallpaper for mobile phones and Firefox browser window designs (Personas). Enjoy the next generation of the Open Web by creating your own wallpapers!

Exciting features of Foxkeh's Wallpaper Creator

Foxkeh's Wallpaper Creator is a tool that allows you to easily create wallpapers that are themed on Foxkeh, which is Firefox’s mascot character born in Japan. In addition to desktop wallpapers for PCs and standby wallpapers on cell phones, it allows you to create designs for Personas with which you can transform the look of the Firefox web browser.
This tool has been developed using a web-based technology called SVG. It allows the user to freely edit images and perform more creative tasks on the browser without installing a plug-in. Enjoy the process of making your own wallpapers using a web technology that is one step ahead of the time.
The program is very simple to use. First, you choose the type of wallpaper you would like to create. Then select from various backgrounds and a range of images of Foxkeh striking different poses. Performing some editing work, such as resizing Foxkeh and moving him around, allows you to come up with your very own design. All you have left to do is to adjust the settings.
Are you ready to experience the ever-evolving world of web? Let’s get started on creating your own wallpaper!

What's SVG?

SVG stands for Scalable Vector Graphics and refers to the graphic format that memorizes the shapes of images and figures.
It is drawing much attention as the web-based technology for the next generation. JPEG and PNG are the most commonly used graphic formats. However, once they are saved as data, the images cannot be resized without impacting the quality nor can they be edited partially. It is sometimes difficult to make a small image bigger while trying to maintain its definition.
With SVG technology, the lines come out smooth no matter how small or large you change the graphics into and the position and size of the graphics and figures can be changed freely.
Cherishing the web-based technology and focusing on the potential of SVG, Mozilla has been supporting this technology for five years.
